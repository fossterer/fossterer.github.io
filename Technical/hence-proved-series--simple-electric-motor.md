Parts needed:

* 1 AA battery -- That's the power source
* 2 safety pins -- You know they have wide surface that can be taped to sides of our battery and have loops on the other side and oh, they're conductors
* 22 AWG Magnetic wire -- well, AWG is a way to measure thickness and all we need is a conducting wire that can be shaped into a coil with bare hands
* 220grit sandpaper -- because though it is called magnetic wire, the coating over it prevents electricity 🤷
* Tape or sewing thread -- because something has to keep our safety pins attached to those battery terminals 
* one or more Neodymium magnets -- any small disc magnets so they can be distant from the rotating coil
* (Optional) Multimeter -- so you can test the build as you go

Process:

1. Loop the conducting wire over the battery to turn it into a coil but with long ends sticking out on either side
2. Sand entirely one end of the sticking out wire but only the bottom portion of the other end. Here, bottom or top don't mean much as long as you sand only half of the wire surface
2. Tape or tie with a thread the bulky ends of safety pins to both sides of the battery
3. If you have a Multimeter handy, test the small  loops on the other side of the safety pins to ensure the electricity is flowing. At the same time, you can check if the sanded ends of wire are truly conducting
4. Place the magnets -- 1 or more stacked on the battery
5. Balance the 2 sticking ends of the coil in the small loops of safety pins
6. Start rotating one end of the coil slowly. You should feel a tension. That's the magents attracting back/repelling the electromagnet formed inside our conducting loop!
7. Spin a bit more and the spin never ends. There's the electric motor

Troubleshooting:

1. Often, the rotation doesn't hold for long. Your wire resting inside loops of safety pin might be uneven. In the absence of perfect balance, you can try tilting the setup while holding in hand.
2. Though you think you taped well, the safety pin ends might have lost contact with the battery ends. A Multimeter helps ensuring conductivity in this case
